02

General Direction

Art Direction

Real estate is a sector that’s been visually coasting on the same moodboard for about a decade. Stock architecture, neutral palette, “premium” in the tagline but nothing in the design to back it up. The brief was to build something that couldn’t be confused with any of that, which meant committing to space, to type that carries weight, and to not adding things just because it feels safer to have them there.

01

Hero Section

We commited to a 3D-modelled skyline that reacts to the cursor, because we wanted to catch the eye and to create something that was different from the usual architecture photo, while keeping the same universe. With the 3D reacting to the cursor, the hook isn’t visual, it’s behavioral, and helps detaching it from every other real estate site at first glance.

02

Explanations Section

Website

The offer has a lot of layers, and layers are where most websites lose people : they pile everything into one block and call it a page. We gave each argument its own room. One idea, full stop. It both serves the message and looks cool as hell.

03

Conversion Section

Website

Trust doesn’t come from a headline that says “trusted.” It comes from evidence, real quotes, real numbers. The whole section is built around answering the silent question every prospect carries : does this actually work for someone like me?

04

Mojo Section

Website

The scroll-reveal uncovers the values one line at a time as you move through the page. It does not only look cool and reactive to everyone’s own pace, but it also helps reading the whole thing without getting bored.
